## Email tracking

Newsletters contain a **tracking pixel** — a small invisible image that records when an email is opened. This is industry-standard practice and helps me understand whether newsletters are being received. Note that some email clients (notably Apple Mail) block or proxy these requests, making open tracking unreliable.

Newsletters also contain **tracked links**. When you click a link, your click is recorded before you are redirected to the destination. Only the fact of the click and the link destination are recorded — no browsing behaviour beyond the click itself.

If you prefer not to be tracked in this way, you can disable image loading in your email client, which will prevent open tracking. Click tracking cannot be disabled without unsubscribing.
